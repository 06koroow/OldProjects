QA Manual

As a Veeve field team member, you are a direct representation of Veeve and Veeve’s image to our guests and hosts.
As such please ensure you:

Do

  • keep your clothing and shoes are always neat, tidy, and presentable when on shift.
  • keep a business casual style as you may meet hosts and guests alike.
  • Keep hair tidy and brushed and keep your nails well-groomed.
  • Wear comfortable/walking shoes

Don’t

  • Sporty clothing or shoes and accessories, and clothing.
  • Shabby hair or beard,
  • Long unkempt nails,
Click the link for more info about our business casual dress code.

Your are required to have this information in hand before your day starts for each QA:

  • Booking ID
  • Property ID (which we mostly using, number which each house is tagged)
  • Key ID (number which is key tagged for each house)
  • Full property address
  • Exact time of cleaning duration ( in this time QA visit must happened)
  • Initial of Account manager
  • CI (Check in) green label
  • CO (check out) red label
  • B2B (Check out+check in same day) usually orange label
  • Access details (Where and how-to pick-up keys with KeyNest collection code)
  • KeyNest is smart key exchange (Usually keys stored in off licence shop). On picking up you must give collection code (6 digital numbers) and you will get keys. In the keys code is wrong or invalid please ask Ops for new. If you must make new KeyNest (KN) tag (ops will give you 6 digital number code which you will pass to person in charge to make).
  • ALARM INSTRUCTION (before each attending to property make sure you read instruction and understood how to follow step by step to deactivate or activate alarm system)

    • This information can be found in the Home manual, Trello, Lavanda and PMS. If you are unsure where to find information, please contact your team leader or the Ops Co-Ordinator for guidance

All information important for QA about house (How to access to property, alarm instruction, request from host, request from Ops, request from guests, important all the time read description and follow what is there) Follow the link under the text for better view how house should look like after cleaning and after QA visit (This means furniture in same position how is on website, and house look like almost same how is on website, this is important on guest arriving to have a good first impression the house is actual house which they book and see over the website). This ensure that the property matches with its pics on the website- so it is what the guest expect to see. Should this not be the case, please inform Ops.

  • WI-FI password
  • important section about boiler (how to operate boiler, change hot water and heating)
  • Information about rubbish collection and collection days
  • Where is store hairdryer, iron, iron boar, fuse box
  • And more useful information for guest (about places to visit, restaurants, transportation)

Tape to protect and secure personal host belongings during guest staying in their houses. Sometimes already in houses will be applied tape (host already secure personal belongings) in that case DO NOT OPEN

HOW TO USE AND WHEN:

When:

  • You find personal belongings in cupboard, drawers, all around house, which must be stored in safe place and tape, so guest does not have access to touch.
  • In house some value stuff (laptops, phones, jewellery, money, expensive perfumes, creams, hats, clothes, shoes etc.

IMPORTANT: Make many pictures of valuable stuff and video (As a proof in case of some situation, complaints etc. )

Actions:

  • Store all valuable or stuff which your personal judgement and opinion is not for guest use (can be stolen, damaged, expensive) in cupboard, drawer or safe place
  • Take picture where you store stuff
  • Close cupboard, drawer and put tamper tape
  • Take a picture after task is completed

What to do when a tamper taped cupboard has been opened:

  • Take a picture of opened tape
  • Take a picture from inside
  • Replace tape with new one and take a picture

Always check the house manual note to find out the council collection day.
Refer to the following instructions to find out when to request a private rubbish collection.

When to order a private rubbish collection:

At the CI ( CHECK-IN):
If the rubbish bins are more than half full and the collection day is more than 3 days away. (you need to make a judgement call and understand if the guest will have enough space in the bin for their rubbish before the next collection)
At the CO (CHECK-OUT)
  • The rubbish bins are completely full (all of them) and the collection day is not the day after.
  • The rubbish bins are completely full and there are some extra bags outside the bins (either recycling or normal)

Please, note that you are not allowed to place the rubbish bins at the pavement in the morning/ afternoon even if the collection day is the day after. You can move them closer to the pavement but within the property gate.

Elements that must be photographed in your report:

  • CLEANLINESS AND PRESENTATION Show surface clutter and dirt free, beds made, towels and cushion placed correctly, show that our standards have been followed.
  • SAFETY AND SECURITY OF THE HOUSE Show that the house is hazard free, doors and windows are locked and double locked, alarm set
  • FUNCTIONALITY Show that all appliances are working. Take pics of the lights on and other appliances on (take a pic of appliances, after you tested them, showing your switch them off! i.e. hobs and oven).
    Take a pics of boiler and thermostat, as well as of the internet speed test floor by floor and room by room.
  • ACCURACY Show that the house matches its listing on our website.
  • STORAGE SPACE Show that the house has got enough space for our guest
  • DAMAGES Show the damages that you find at the property. Add your comments to help us understand in which room they are.
  • HOME CONDITION Show the general condition of the home, Wear & Tear and add your suggestion

    • PICTURES

    • Start from the top floor and the right hand side
    • Take 4 pics, one per each corner of each room and 1 pic of the ceiling and one of the floor. Those are meant to be panoramic pics of the room
    • Proceed by taking close up pics of each room that include all the elements mentioned above.
    • Add you comment to describe damages, issues or anything that we need to know.
    • Take pics of all the appliances, on and off where possible.
    • Take pics of windows, doors locked and alarm armed.
    • Take pics of storage space
    • Take pics of the tamper taped areas
    • Take pics of the outside space and garden
    • Take pic of the internet speed tests per each floor
    • Take pic of thermostat and boiler
    • Take pics of the rubbish outside

  • add the CLEANING SCORE
  • Tag the relevant AM in any damges, issues or suggestions to be passed to the host
  • MARK THE REPORT AS COMPLETED.

    • HEATING AND HOT WATER WINTER SEASON

  • On CI make sure heating and hot water working properly ( leave thermostat on 21 degrees after your visit). Always check the size of the house, in some cases you will need to raise the temperature. The aim is to ensure the guest checks in in a warm house, ensure you switch the heating on when you arrive at the property so there is enough time for the house to warm up.
  • On CO make sure following instructions from HM.
  • B2B /PC please check when guest arriving if is in next 2-3 days leave heating on 18 degrees,
  • in case we are preparing house in advance (CI for 4-5 days from date of cleaning) make sure you set heating and hot water as on CO following the HM instruction.
